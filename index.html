Autobiography

I like to joke that I haven't gone very far in life. I still live in my childhood home, attend my childhood church, and I was even elected to the same New York State Assembly seat my father held when I was a child. My shoes are stuck in Webster.

I graduated from Boston College and Wake Forest University School of Law, and throughout my career I have had the opportunity to serve in both the public and private sectors. I worked as a felony trial prosecutor, served as a Monroe County Legislator, and represented our community in the New York State Assembly. I also represented New York State on the international Great Lakes Commission and served as Director of the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation.

In the private sector, I founded and operated health care, manufacturing, and real estate companies that created hundreds of jobs in Monroe County. I was also a partner at Rochester's largest downtown law firm.

Most importantly, Webster has always been home. My wife, Marlene, and I have been married for 24 years, and we are blessed with two children and two grandchildren, all of whom live in Monroe County. I have also spent many years coaching Rochester Youth Hockey and CYO basketball at St. Rita's- experiences that have meant a great deal to me and kept me closely connected to Webster.
